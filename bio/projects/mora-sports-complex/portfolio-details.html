Multi-Purpose Indoor Sports Facility

The Mora Sports Complex is a four-storey, multi-functional indoor sports facility designed to address the absence of a swimming pool and centralized indoor sports infrastructure at the University of Moratuwa. The complex integrates sports, fitness, recreation, and spectator facilities within a compact footprint while maintaining safety, accessibility, and sustainability.

The project emphasizes:

  • Efficient space planning
  • Clear structural load paths
  • Multi-sport adaptability
  • Compliance with Sri Lankan building codes
  • Sustainable and economical material usage

Project Objectives

  • Provide a semi-Olympic swimming pool within university premises
  • Accommodate multiple indoor sports in a single structure
  • Ensure unobstructed spectator views using optimized structural systems
  • Promote accessibility and user safety
  • Integrate sustainable design strategies

Design & Engineering Considerations

Structural System

  • Reinforced Concrete (RCC) Frame Structure
  • Designed to resist gravity and lateral loads (wind & seismic).
  • Transfer beams used to increase column spacing and reduce visual obstructions.
  • Pre-tensioned beams for large spans.

Digital & Physical Modelling

CAD & BIM Work: Developed full 3D architectural & structural models using Revit, with floor-wise BIM modelling and spatial coordination. 2D AutoCAD drawings (Scale 1:1) were also produced.

Physical Model: Created a 1:120 scale model using foam boards, wooden sticks (for structure), and paper sheets to represent load transfer paths, beam-column connections, and lateral stability.
